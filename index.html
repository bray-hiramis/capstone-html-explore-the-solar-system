The Outer Planets

The giant planets in the outer solar system don't have hard surfaces. Instead, they have swirling gases above a core. Jupiter and Saturn are gas giants. Uranus and Neptune are ice giants.

Famous Space Missions

Apollo 11:
This mission, launched on July 16, 1969, achieved the goal of landing humans on the Moon, with Neil Armstrong and Buzz Aldrin becoming the first to walk on its surface.

Apollo 13:
Known for its dramatic near-disaster, Apollo 13's mission was a tense ordeal for the astronauts, the ground crew, and the world.

Voyager Probes:
The Voyager 1 and 2 probes, launched in the late 1970s, have traversed vast distances, providing invaluable scientific data about our solar system and beyond.

Hubble Space Telescope:
Launched in 1990, it provided astronomers with a powerful new tool for studying the Universe.

Cassini-Huygens Mission:
A long-term multinational space exploration project that explored Saturn and its moons, including Titan.
